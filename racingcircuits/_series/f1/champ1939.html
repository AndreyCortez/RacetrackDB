FORMULA 1 WORLD CHAMPIONSHIP

1939 A.I.A.C.R. European Grand Prix Driver's Championship

The following is courtesy of Hans Etzrodt and posted originally on the Atlas F1 Nostalgia Bulletin Board.

 

  • There was the official 1939 European Championship in place by the A.I.A.C.R., which were undecided of how to determine the champion after the conclusion of the four championship runs. They never awarded the title. Therefore there was no 1939 European Champion.
  • Because the A.I.A.C.R. did not award the title, the German ONS declared Hermann Lang as 1939 European Champion without providing details. This was a concoction contrived by the German Third Reich propaganda machine and should have been unacceptable to all other nations.
  • After 1945, most authoritative writers have been either ignorant of the above facts or have not noticed them, and have only noted Muller as having more points at best.
