AAA National Championship Revisions

The original AAA Championship began in 1916 and was won by Dario Resta. The 1917 Championship was cancelled due to the United States' participation in World War I. The Championship was restarted in 1920 and held over 5 events, with the title awarded posthumously to Gaston Chevrolet.

In 1926 Val Haresnape, Secretary of the AAA Contest Board, amended the 1920 championship, counting all 11 AAA sanctioned events held in 1920 and thus awarding the title to Tommy Milton. At this time, champions were created for the years 1909 to 1919 where previously no championship was held.

In 1951 Russ Catlin created champions for the years 1902 to 1909, suggesting George Robertson as the 1909 champion and also giving the 1920 championship to Tommy Milton.
