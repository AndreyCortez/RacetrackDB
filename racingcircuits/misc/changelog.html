History

This site started as a copy of Dan Kings racingcircuits.net. The original went offline sometime in 2005, and was then copied at least 3 times. One of these copies lived here on the Fastlane for posterity thanks to PTRACER. Needless to say all credits for the original site goes to Dan King and/or people otherwise mentioned. Dans original about page can be found here

For any questions regarding the hosting of the original site contact PTRACER via the link above.

As time went on the site grew ever more outdated and a slight alteration to Circuit the Catalunya grew into a full fledged project to give the site a total renovation. However old and outdated it may have been to me it still was the best resource out there for circuits, especially for historic venues.

Currently I'm swapping out old pages for new/updated ones a circuit at the time. This involves an upgrade from HTML1 to HTML5 and all the features that come with it. Since I'm doing this from the bottom up there will be an accumulation of broken links for quite some while. I try to keep the main country listings working but for everything else I'll get there when I get there.

In order to help increase the accuracy of the site, your help is needed.

I come across many instances of incomplete information on sites used to compile the individual pages. While the aim is to provide as accurate information as possible, at a point I have to put out pages with incomplete information, else I'd be stuck on a track forever and nothing gets done. Generally if there is a question mark the information needs clarification, see Further Details at the bottom of the page. That is a long way of saying, if you find something inaccurate or missing and you can fill in the gap hop over to the forum and give us a word.

I'm also grateful for any material on not yet updated or missing circuits, such as track maps, aerial photographs, etc

Michkov

About

The RCDB provides a visual history of racing venues from the beginning of motorsport to the present day. While the database aims to be complete, it isn't at the current moment and may most likely never be. Yet the aim is to be the most complete and authorative site of it's kind.

The circuits are organized by country, as well as by series (that feature is broken at the moment though). Within them the tracks are organized by type with the main variant taking precedent. At the individual track page you'll find the various track layouts plus some basic stats for each. Below the the track maps there are generally 2 rows of buttons. The first selects the year a major change was made to the track the second one picks a specific layout for that period. Below the statblock there are further infos and open questions for the track. Finally sources are found at the bottom.

Misc notes:

  • Official lengths are used whereever they can be found, but minor layouts are rarely given distances or are plainly wrong so most of them are computed from the length of a layout that is known. Given that all maps are dran using Inkscape, I'm fairly certain the errors are low enough to be negligiable.
