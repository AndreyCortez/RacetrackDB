No racing took place during 1917-1918 and 1942-1945 due to the World Wars.
The Red Bull Air Race used a standing start from the central straight. The course consisted of 3 laps which is the display distance.
The oval was initially crushed stone covered in tar, which proved to be inadequate to the strains of racing. The surface was paved with bricks after the first meeting. The brick surface was replaced piecemeal over the next 60 years.
