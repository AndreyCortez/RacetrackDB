Further Details
The California Automobile Club held their 1904 meeting in Del Monte, which included races at the Del Monte (horse) track and a hillclimb on the last day. This page is for the hillclimb. Unfortunately the sources are not clear which route was taken. All information I have is 1 5/8ths miles length, a gradient of 7-12% (unsure if that is maximum or average), it going up Carmel Hill.
The three routes presented here are my best guesses. The are all approximately 1.625 miles long and have a gradient within the above limits.
I have no idea where Carmel Hill is. It is not on any map of the period and modern ones point towards Carmel at the Sea, or the top where all the finishes are. Although the latter seems to a modern place name. Period sources mention the Carmel Valley so I tend to favour the southern two routes as the most likely.
