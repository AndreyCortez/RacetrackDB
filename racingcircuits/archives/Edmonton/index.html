Edmonton International Speedway, Alberta, Canada

Type: Permanent Road Course
Length: 2.527 Miles (GP Course) / 1.500 Miles (Medium Course) / 0.875 Miles (Short Course)
Used: 1968 - 1983

This road course in Edmonton, Alberta was first used for a round of the Can-Am championship in September of 1968. The circuit utilised parts of the drag strip which had been inaugurated a year earlier. The first few years of operation where to be the most successful for the the circuit, as in addition to the Can-Am series, events were held for the Trans-Am, Formula 5000 and Formula Atlantic championships. The circuit was closed in 1983 and most of the site has now been redeveloped as a housing estate.
